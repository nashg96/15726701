Deep-Level Processing W3.CSS
Deep processing is a way of learning in which you try to make the information meaningful to yourself. For example, you might try to figure out how a lesson on animal biology fits into what you already know about your dog.

Here are three ways to process information deeply:
1.Elaborate on the information.
When you are given new information, begin by asking yourself how this relates to what you already know. This creates pathways where the new information can “live” in your brain.
2.Make distinctions
You put the information side-by-side with other concepts and ask yourself what the key differences are?
3.Apply it to your life
Try using the information in real life. For instance, if you are studying human anatomy, try to name the bones you use when you brush your teeth or ride a bike.
